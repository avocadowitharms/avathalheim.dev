Launching is different

Launching is different

There is a strange moment when an app stops being a folder on your machine and starts becoming something other people can install.

It's something you built.

Something you spent time, thought, and effort on.

Something you're emotionally attached to.

You start thinking about things you barely noticed before.

First impressions.

Screenshots.

Margins.

The size of a button.

You question the icon.

The name.

The UI.

You wonder if people will understand what you're trying to build.

Or if they'll care at all.

At some point, you realize you're not just shipping software.

You're exposing a small part of yourself to the public.

Building the app is the easy part.

Showing it to the world is where things get real.
