## From project to product

There is a strange moment when an app stops being a folder on your machine and starts being something other people can install.

The code did not magically become more impressive when it reached the App Store, but the product became more real. Screenshots mattered. Copy mattered. Privacy answers mattered. The icon mattered more than I wanted it to.

## The parts I underestimated

- Preparing store assets takes longer than expected
- Privacy labels force useful product clarity
- TestFlight feedback feels different from local testing
- Small edge cases become louder near release

## What I would do earlier next time

I would create the store listing earlier in the process. Not because distribution is the main thing, but because it exposes unanswered product questions.

If I cannot explain the app clearly on one store page, there is probably still some thinking left to do inside the product.
